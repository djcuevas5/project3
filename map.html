Facts about Batangas

  • Taal Volcano Located in the province of Batangas, Taal volcano have erupted 38 times through history, making it the second most active volcano in the Philippines
  • Batangas Lomi Lomi Batangas is a Batangas-specific lomi variation, primarily made from pork meat, liver, rind, chicharon, kikiam, and fried pork belly, with vegetables used only as garnish.
  • Batangas Tagalog Batangas Tagalog is a dialect of the Tagalog language, primarily spoken in Batangas and parts of Cavite, Quezon, Laguna, and Mindoro. It has a strong accent and vocabulary closely related to Old Tagalog. The dialect uses the passive imperfect instead of the present progressive tense, unlike Manila Tagalog. Batangas Tagalog also uses the verb ending -i instead of -an mo, especially in the imperative, when the verb stands alone or is the last word in a phrase.
